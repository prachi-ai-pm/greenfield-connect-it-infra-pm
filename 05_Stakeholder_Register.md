## 3. Detailed Stakeholder Profiles

### COO (Executive Sponsor)
- **Interest:** Meeting the contractually committed customer go-live date without penalty exposure
- **Influence:** Final approval authority on charter, budget variance, and go/no-go
- **Concerns raised:** None recorded as resistance; set the explicit 24-hour escalation condition at charter approval
- **Engagement:** Weekly status visibility; immediate escalation path for any go-live risk

### Head of IT Infrastructure (Delivery Sponsor)
- **Interest:** Standard architecture compliance; long-term supportability of the branch
- **Influence:** Approved architecture deviations (24U rack); owns the company's reference architecture standard
- **Engagement:** Architecture gate sign-off (Stage 3); ongoing delivery sponsorship

### Regional Operations Director
- **Interest:** Accurate headcount/seating sizing; mid-build operational needs (e.g., conference room)
- **Influence:** Provided the real headcount and seating data used to size network/Wi-Fi/telephony capacity
- **Concerns raised:** Requested an additional conference room with network/AV drops mid-build, routed through formal change control and approved with zero schedule impact
- **Engagement:** Discovery interview (Stage 1); change-request approval loop (Stage 4)

### Facilities
- **Interest:** Building base-build completion (electrical, HVAC)
- **Influence:** Co-owned the site-readiness definition with IT; provided the landlord handover documents
- **Concerns raised:** Initially held a different definition of "done" than IT (power/HVAC live vs. full network connectivity verified) — resolved via a jointly signed-off site-readiness checklist
- **Engagement:** Discovery site walkthrough (Stage 1); joint readiness sign-off (ongoing through Stage 4)

### Security Lead
- **Interest:** Baseline compliance, segmentation, guest isolation
- **Influence:** Found the guest Wi-Fi isolation gap before install and required a redesign before sign-off
- **Engagement:** Requirements gathering (Stage 2); architecture walkthrough and sign-off (Stage 3); pre-go-live security baseline audit (Stage 5)

### Regional Helpdesk Manager
- **Interest:** Operational supportability after the project team stands down
- **Influence:** Final handover acceptance authority; flagged a real on-call coverage gap (no engineer trained on the new telephony platform)
- **Concerns raised:** On-call gap held the handover open for an additional week until two engineers completed vendor training — accepted as the right trade-off rather than a missed deadline
- **Engagement:** Handover walkthrough and acceptance sign-off (Stage 7); ongoing operational threshold ownership post-closure

### Structured Cabling Contractor
- **Interest:** Contract execution, schedule and staffing management
- **Influence:** Initial progress reporting (70% claimed vs. ~45% actual) materially affected schedule confidence
- **Concerns raised:** Found to be short-staffed; resolved by bringing in a second crew at the contractor's cost under the existing contract terms, after the PM requested an as-built, run-by-run status report
- **Engagement:** Certified site survey (Stage 2); twice-weekly site coordination meetings and direct progress verification (Stage 4)

### WAN Circuit Provider
- **Interest:** Standard delivery lead time and SLA terms
- **Influence:** A 7-week realistic delivery estimate against a 12-week working window made this the single highest schedule risk in the project
- **Engagement:** Vendor evaluation (Stage 2); circuit ordered immediately on provisional address verification, with an LTE failover router arranged as a documented contingency

### Branch Lead (On-Site, Coimbatore)
- **Interest:** Smooth day-one operations for staff
- **Influence:** Primary point of contact during hypercare; surfaced the VoIP adoption/awareness gap during daily check-ins
- **Engagement:** Daily 8am check-ins during first-week hypercare (Stage 6)

## 4. Stakeholder Engagement Cadence Summary

| Engagement | Frequency | Stakeholders |
|---|---|---|
| RAID log review | Weekly | PM, Sponsor |
| Site coordination meetings | Twice weekly | PM, contractors, engineers |
| Go-live risk escalation | Within 24 hours of identification | PM → COO |
| Hypercare check-ins | Daily (first week post-go-live) | PM, Branch Lead |
| Operational threshold review | Weekly (uptime, ticket volume); Quarterly (capacity, UPS health) | Regional Helpdesk, Network Engineer, Facilities + IT |
| 30-day post-go-live review | Once, at the 30-day mark | PM, Sponsor |
